# GATAF Immutable Principles

**Governed AI Team Acceleration Framework**
*Version: Pre-v1 | Author: Richard "Dick" Wilson (@idothuman)*

---

## About These Principles

The nine principles below are the **immutable foundation** of GATAF. They represent the non-negotiable governance commitments of the framework.

> ⚠️ **These principles may not be modified, overridden, or removed without explicit direction from the project owner.**

All team-level standards, templates, rituals, and measurements must be consistent with these principles. Where a proposed change conflicts with a principle, the principle takes precedence.

---

## The Nine Principles

---

### Principle 1 – Corporate Policies as Foundation

**Statement:** All GATAF standards must derive from and be consistent with the corporate policies applicable to the team and organization.

**Rationale:** AI governance cannot exist in a vacuum. Enterprise teams operate within a policy hierarchy that includes regulatory requirements, HR policies, data governance standards, and security frameworks. GATAF standards are a *specification* of those policies into operational team guidance — not a replacement for them.

**Implication:** Before a team creates any AI guardrail, they must identify the corporate policy it is derived from. Guardrails without policy anchoring are not valid GATAF standards.

---

### Principle 2 – No Invalidation Rule

**Statement:** Lower-level standards created within GATAF cannot override, weaken, or invalidate higher-level corporate or regulatory policy.

**Rationale:** Teams may be tempted to create convenient AI usage standards that conflict with corporate governance. The No Invalidation Rule prevents this by establishing a strict hierarchy: corporate policy > GATAF framework standards > team-level guardrails.

**Implication:** The Policy Validation Engine (a supporting GATAF engine) is responsible for ensuring this principle is enforced at each standard-creation event.

---

### Principle 3 – Framework Validation Mandatory

**Statement:** All major outputs produced with AI assistance must be validated against the Policy Validation Checklist before use, distribution, or publication.

**Rationale:** "Major outputs" include any deliverable that will leave the team context (customer-facing, executive-facing, legally sensitive, or compliance-relevant). Validation ensures that the output is traceable, attributable, and compliant.

**Implication:** Teams must define what constitutes a "major output" in their Team Playbook. The validation checklist is not optional for these items.

---

### Principle 4 – Transparency

**Statement:** Every AI contribution to a team output must be explainable by a team member in under 30 seconds.

**Rationale:** AI "black boxes" erode trust, create compliance risk, and cannot be audited. Transparency is not just ethical — it is operational. If a team member cannot explain what AI did and why, the AI contribution must be revised or removed.

**Implication:** The AI Disclosure Protocol (a key artifact) operationalizes this principle. All AI-assisted outputs carry a disclosure label and a brief explanation of AI's role.

---

### Principle 5 – On-Demand Status Determinability

**Statement:** The status of any work item within the GATAF framework must be instantly determinable by any authorized team member at any time.

**Rationale:** Governance requires visibility. If work items can become "lost," "unclear," or "ambiguous in status," the framework cannot maintain accountability. This principle enforces a commitment to transparent work tracking.

**Implication:** Teams must use a consistent status labeling system for all work items. The Status Label Metadata System (a key artifact) provides the standard vocabulary.

---

### Principle 6 – Inclusive Ideation

**Statement:** Ideas for framework improvement, new collaboration patterns, or process changes may be captured and submitted via any natural medium by any team member.

**Rationale:** Good ideas come from everywhere and in every form — a voice note in the car, a sketch on a whiteboard, a message in a team chat. The framework must not create barriers to idea capture that filter out valuable contributions.

**Implication:** The Ideation Intake Engine (a supporting GATAF engine) provides the mechanism. Teams should establish a low-friction intake process that accepts any format.

---

### Principle 7 – Emergent Adaptation

**Statement:** The framework expects and encourages new human–AI collaboration patterns to emerge. These patterns should be identified, named, documented, and fed back into the framework.

**Rationale:** Human–AI collaboration is new and evolving. No framework designed today can anticipate all the patterns that will emerge as teams gain experience. Locking the framework against adaptation would make it obsolete.

**Implication:** The Emergent Pattern Discovery Engine (a supporting GATAF engine) watches for new patterns. Teams are encouraged to name and document patterns they observe and submit them as framework contributions.

---

### Principle 8 – Synergy Pursuit

**Statement:** Every design decision within the framework should be evaluated against whether it increases Human–AI–Team Synergy.

**Rationale:** The framework exists to produce outcomes superior to humans alone, AI alone, or unstructured collaboration. If a proposed standard, ritual, or template reduces synergy (by adding friction without governance benefit), it should be revised.

**Implication:** Synergy is a measurable outcome (Layer 5 – Measurement & Feedback). Framework contributors are expected to consider synergy impact when proposing changes.

---

### Principle 9 – Open Source Intent

**Statement:** The GATAF framework is and will remain an open-source contribution to the human–AI collaboration ecosystem, released under the Apache License 2.0.

**Rationale:** Governance frameworks are most valuable when they are widely adopted, tested, and improved by diverse teams. Open-source release under Apache 2.0 ensures the framework can be freely used, adapted, and contributed to while preserving attribution.

**Implication:** All contributions to this repository must be compatible with Apache 2.0 licensing. The [`NOTICE`](../NOTICE) file and [`INVENTORS_DISCLOSURE.md`](../INVENTORS_DISCLOSURE.md) must be preserved in all distributions.
