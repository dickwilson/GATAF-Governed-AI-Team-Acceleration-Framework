# GATAF Framework Overview

**Governed AI Team Acceleration Framework**
*Version: Pre-v1 | Status: Concept Framework | Author: Richard "Dick" Wilson (@idothuman)*

---

## Introduction

GATAF is an open governance framework that enables enterprise teams to safely integrate AI into their daily workflows. It is not a software platform, not a vendor product, and not a replacement for existing tools. It is a **meta-framework** — a structured approach to thinking about, governing, and measuring how humans and AI work together inside real organizations.

The framework was created in response to a consistent pattern observed in enterprise AI adoption: teams want to use AI, leadership wants AI to deliver value, but no one has clearly defined *how* AI should participate in team work, what boundaries exist, and how compliance with corporate governance is maintained.

GATAF solves this by providing a repeatable, auditable, policy-anchored operating model for human–AI collaboration.

---

## Core Premise

> *AI adoption fails not because the technology isn't ready — but because the governance isn't.*

Enterprise teams need:

1. **Clarity** on what AI can and cannot do within their context
2. **Standards** that are grounded in existing corporate policy
3. **Rituals** that make AI collaboration a structured, predictable activity
4. **Transparency** that lets any stakeholder understand AI's role in any output
5. **Measurement** that demonstrates value and flags drift

GATAF provides all five.

## Primary Outcome: Human–AI–Team Synergy

The framework is designed to produce outcomes superior to:

- **Humans working alone** — AI augments speed, scale, and pattern recognition
- **AI working alone** — Human judgment, ethics, and context remain irreplaceable
- **Unstructured human–AI collaboration** — Governance prevents errors, bias, and compliance drift

This triangulated superiority is what GATAF calls **Human–AI–Team Synergy**, and it is the primary measurable target of the framework.

## The Five-Layer Architecture

GATAF is organized into five sequential but iterative layers:

### Layer 1 – Work Genome Mapping
Before AI can accelerate anything, teams must understand what they actually do. This layer documents:
- All significant work activities
- Inputs and outputs for each activity
- Tools and systems in use
- Applicable corporate policies per activity
- Current pain points and bottlenecks

**Output:** A completed Work Genome Map for the team

### Layer 2 – Acceleration Opportunity Scan
With work mapped, teams assess each activity for AI acceleration potential:
- Which tasks are repetitive and rule-based?
- Which require judgment that must remain human?
- Where does latency create bottlenecks that AI could reduce?
- Where does policy allow AI participation?

**Output:** A prioritized Acceleration Opportunity Assessment

### Layer 3 – Team Standards & Guardrails
Teams co-create their operational AI governance:
- Acceptable AI behaviors per work type
- Human review and sign-off requirements
- AI disclosure rules (what must be disclosed and when)
- Escalation paths for edge cases

**Output:** Team AI Standards Document (derived from the Team Playbook Template)

### Layer 4 – Implementation Rituals
Governance is only effective if it's practiced. This layer defines:
- Daily collaboration check-ins (human reviews AI drafts)
- Weekly synergy reviews (what worked, what didn't)
- Monthly policy validation sweeps
- Quarterly pattern discovery sessions

**Output:** Active team rituals embedded in team cadence

### Layer 5 – Measurement & Feedback
What gets measured gets managed:
- Transparency compliance rate
- AI disclosure completeness
- Synergy outcome scores
- Policy validation pass rates
- Adoption velocity

**Output:** Living dashboard / tracking artifact per team

## The Three Supporting Engines

These systems operate across all layers continuously:

### Ideation Intake Engine
Ensures no ideas are lost. Team members capture ideas in any format — voice note, sticky note, chat message, email — and the engine routes them into the framework's backlog for evaluation.

### Emergent Pattern Discovery Engine
Human–AI collaboration will produce new, unexpected patterns. This engine watches for them, names them, and feeds them back into the framework's rituals and standards — enabling the framework to evolve.

### Policy Validation Engine
At any point, any output can be run through the Policy Validation Engine to check compliance with corporate governance. This provides the "audit trail" that enterprise governance teams require.

## Framework Governance

The framework is governed by nine immutable principles. See [`principles.md`](principles.md) for full detail.

In summary: all team-level standards must derive from corporate policy, AI contributions must be transparently disclosed, and the framework must measurably increase human–AI–team synergy.

## How to Adopt GATAF

1. Assign a **Framework Owner** on the team (human, accountable)
2. Complete the **Work Genome Template** (Layer 1)
3. Run the **Acceleration Opportunity Scan** (Layer 2)
4. Draft your **Team Playbook** (Layer 3)
5. Activate your **Implementation Rituals** (Layer 4)
6. Establish your **Measurement Baseline** (Layer 5)
7. Run cycles, capture patterns, improve

No new tooling required. No vendor engagement required. No lengthy procurement cycle.
