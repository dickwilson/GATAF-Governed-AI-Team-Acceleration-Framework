# GATAF Framework Overview

> **GATAF – Governed AI Team Acceleration Framework**
> Version: Pre-v1 | Status: Concept Framework | License: Apache 2.0

---

## What Is GATAF?

GATAF is a lightweight, open governance framework that helps enterprise teams safely and transparently integrate AI into their daily work. It provides the structure, standards, and rituals needed to move from unmanaged AI experimentation to governed, measurable human–AI collaboration.

GATAF operates **inside existing team infrastructure**. It does not require a new platform, tooling purchase, or organizational restructuring. It works by layering governance, transparency, and operational patterns onto the work teams are already doing.

---

## Why GATAF Exists

Most enterprise teams today either:

- **Avoid AI entirely** due to unclear governance and policy uncertainty
- **Use AI informally** without standards, documentation, or oversight
- **Experience inconsistent results** because collaboration patterns are undefined

Neither extreme produces durable value. GATAF provides a middle path: a structured framework that enables AI use while preserving corporate policy compliance, transparency, and human accountability.

---

## The Primary Outcome: Human–AI–Team Synergy

GATAF is built around a single north star metric:

> **Human–AI–Team Synergy** — a state in which the combined effort of humans and AI, guided by GATAF governance, produces results superior to what either could achieve independently or through unstructured collaboration.

This is not just a productivity metric. It encompasses:

- Quality of output
- Speed of delivery
- Policy compliance
- Transparency of AI contribution
- Team learning and adaptation

## How GATAF Works

GATAF operates through five progressive layers and three supporting engines.

### The Five Layers

Each layer builds on the previous and produces artifacts that feed into the next.

**Layer 1 – Work Genome Mapping**
Teams document their current work: what they do, what inputs they use, what tools they rely on, and what policies govern their output. This becomes the team's "Work Genome" — the baseline from which AI acceleration is measured.

**Layer 2 – Acceleration Opportunity Scan**
Using the Work Genome, teams identify which tasks are strong candidates for AI assistance. Tasks are evaluated for AI suitability, policy compatibility, and risk level.

**Layer 3 – Team Standards & Guardrails**
Teams define what acceptable AI use looks like for their context. This includes disclosure protocols, quality thresholds, human review requirements, and escalation paths.

**Layer 4 – Implementation Rituals**
Teams adopt repeating operational patterns — daily stand-ups, weekly synergy reviews, monthly governance retrospectives — that keep human–AI collaboration visible, measurable, and improvable.

**Layer 5 – Measurement & Feedback**
Teams track transparency compliance, synergy outcomes, AI adoption health, and governance adherence. Results feed back into earlier layers to drive continuous improvement.

### The Three Supporting Engines

Three cross-cutting systems operate across all five layers:

| Engine | Function |
|--------|----------|
| Ideation Intake Engine | Captures raw ideas from team members in any format |
| Emergent Pattern Discovery Engine | Detects new collaboration patterns that arise organically |
| Policy Validation Engine | Ensures standards and outputs comply with corporate governance |

## What GATAF Is Not

| GATAF Is | GATAF Is Not |
|----------|--------------|
| A governance framework | A software platform |
| A team-level operating model | An organizational AI strategy |
| A set of templates and rituals | A vendor product |
| Policy-aligned by design | A replacement for corporate policy |
| Human-led | AI-autonomous |

---

## Framework Boundaries

GATAF is intentionally scoped to the **team level**. It is designed to:

- Operate within corporate policy (not define it)
- Complement existing AI tools (not replace them)
- Fit inside current team workflows (not redesign them)
- Scale from a single team to enterprise-wide adoption incrementally
