### What Is GATAF?

**GATAF — Governed AI Team Acceleration Framework** is an open governance framework designed to help enterprise teams safely and transparently integrate AI into their daily workflows. It is:

- **Not a software product** — it's a meta-framework (a structured operating model)
- **Platform-independent** — works inside your existing infrastructure; no new tooling required
- **Policy-anchored** — all standards derive from existing corporate policy
- **Human-primary** — AI is a collaborator, not an authority
- **Open source** — Apache License 2.0

**Primary outcome:** *Human–AI–Team Synergy* — results that exceed what humans alone, AI alone, or unstructured human–AI collaboration can achieve.

### The Problem GATAF Solves

Enterprise AI adoption consistently fails because:

1. Teams have no operational standards for how AI participates in their work
2. Governance is unclear or only exists at the organizational level (not the team level)
3. AI usage transparency is inconsistent — no one knows what the AI contributed to a given output
4. Alignment with corporate policy is difficult because no structured process exists
5. Human–AI collaboration patterns are undefined and ad hoc

GATAF addresses all five by providing a lightweight, repeatable, policy-anchored governance model that operates at the **team level**.

---

### Nine Immutable Principles

These principles are non-negotiable. The framework is built on them:

1. **Corporate Policies as Foundation** — All team-level standards must derive from corporate policy
2. **No Invalidation Rule** — Lower-level standards cannot override higher-level policy
3. **Framework Validation Mandatory** — Major outputs require policy validation before use
4. **Transparency** — AI contributions must be explainable in under 30 seconds (the "30-Second Transparency Standard")
5. **On-Demand Status Determinability** — The status of any work item must be instantly visible
6. **Inclusive Ideation** — Ideas may be captured via any natural medium (voice, text, sketch, etc.)
7. **Emergent Adaptation** — New human–AI collaboration patterns are expected and encouraged
8. **Synergy Pursuit** — Framework design must measurably increase human–AI–team synergy
9. **Open Source Intent** — Released under Apache 2.0 for broad, community-driven adoption

Full detail: https://github.com/dickwilson/GATAF-Governed-AI-Team-Acceleration-Framework/blob/main/framework/principles.md

---

### Five-Layer Architecture

The framework is organized into five sequential but iterative layers:

| Layer | Name | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Work Genome Mapping** | Document what the team actually does — activities, tools, inputs, outputs, policies |
| 2 | **Acceleration Opportunity Scan** | Identify which tasks AI can accelerate; which must stay human-led |
| 3 | **Team Standards & Guardrails** | Co-create governance rules for AI participation, grounded in corporate policy |
| 4 | **Implementation Rituals** | Daily, weekly, monthly, and quarterly collaboration cadences |
| 5 | **Measurement & Feedback** | Track transparency compliance, synergy scores, adoption velocity |

Full detail: https://github.com/dickwilson/GATAF-Governed-AI-Team-Acceleration-Framework/blob/main/framework/layers.md

---

### Three Supporting Engines

These cross-cutting systems operate across all five layers continuously:

| Engine | Function |
|---|---|
| **Ideation Intake Engine** | Captures ideas in any format from any team member; routes them into a reviewed backlog |
| **Emergent Pattern Discovery Engine** | Detects and names new human–AI collaboration patterns as they emerge from practice |
| **Policy Validation Engine** | Validates any standard or output against corporate governance policy; maintains audit trail |
