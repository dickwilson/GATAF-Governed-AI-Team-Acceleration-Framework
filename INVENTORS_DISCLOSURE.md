## Summary of the Framework Concept

GATAF is an open governance framework designed to help enterprise teams safely and transparently integrate AI into their everyday work. The framework addresses a critical gap in enterprise AI adoption: the absence of operational standards, clear governance, and defined human–AI collaboration patterns.

The core innovation of GATAF is the concept of **Human–AI–Team Synergy** — a measurable state in which the combined output of humans and AI, guided by structured governance, exceeds what either could produce independently or through unstructured collaboration.

---

## Key Concepts Originated

### 1. Work Genome Mapping

The idea of treating a team's work activities, inputs, tools, and policies as a structured "genome" that can be documented, analyzed, and mapped for AI acceleration opportunities.

### 2. Five-Layer Framework Architecture

A layered governance model consisting of:

- Layer 1: Work Genome Mapping
- Layer 2: Acceleration Opportunity Scan
- Layer 3: Team Standards & Guardrails
- Layer 4: Implementation Rituals
- Layer 5: Measurement & Feedback

### 3. Three Supporting Engines

Cross-cutting systems that operate across all layers:

- Ideation Intake Engine
- Emergent Pattern Discovery Engine
- Policy Validation Engine

### 4. Immutable Principles

A set of nine non-negotiable governance principles (see [`/framework/principles.md`](framework/principles.md)) that form the foundation of the framework, including the No Invalidation Rule and the 30-Second Transparency Standard.

### 5. Human–AI–Team Synergy as a Primary Metric

The concept of measuring team outcomes not just by productivity, but by the degree to which human–AI collaboration produces results superior to either alone.

## Prior Art Acknowledgment

The originator is aware of existing enterprise AI governance efforts (including but not limited to responsible AI frameworks from major technology vendors). GATAF is distinguished by its focus on:

- Team-level (not just organizational-level) governance
- Operational patterns and rituals, not just policy documents
- Integration within existing infrastructure without requiring new platforms
- Measurable synergy as a first-class framework objective
